What is Phishing?

Phishing is one of the most popular ways for cyber criminals to attack people and organizations. It allows them to steal personal and financial information.

Sending emails is easier than hacking into a department’s firewall, and it is a highly successful way of attacking organizations.

ESDC has one of the largest holdings of personal information in the Government of Canada, making us a prime target for phishing attacks.

Phishing attacks are a real threat to our department. In the last year, there were over 8,000 malicious emails reported by ESDC employees. More than 1,100 of those were suspected phishing attacks. This is despite technology which blocks thousands of more emails every day.

A few hints before you begin your mission…

Pay careful attention to emails if they:

  • Request personal information,
  • Extort or bait you (e.g. send money), or
  • Have a sense of urgency to respond/act on a request (e.g. you have 24 hours to comply).

Other clues that you should watch out for:

  • Generic greeting
  • Time stamp outside normal business hours
  • Spelling and grammatical errors
  • No signature block or contact information

Look

Look for subtle changes to email addresses and links.

Domains should reference a valid organization and be consistent with the message.

  • scammer@hrsdc-rhdcc.goc.ca
  • http://www.servicecanada.gov.ca/eng/home

Cyber criminals often use real subdomains to draw attention away from the domain.

  • scammer@hrsdc-rhdcc this is the subdomain .goc.ca this is the domain
  • http://www.servicecanada this is the subdomain .gov.ca this is the domain /eng/home

Cyber criminals make subtle changes to domains. This is what to watch for.
